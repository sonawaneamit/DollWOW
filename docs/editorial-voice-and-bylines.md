# DollWow Editorial Voice And Bylines

Use this guide for all DollWow SEO guides, Learning Center articles, collection intros, FAQs, and AI content prompts.

## Core Voice

DollWow should sound like a discreet, experienced adult-commerce specialist: practical, calm, premium, and buyer-protective.

The voice should be:

- Clear rather than coy.
- Practical rather than explicit.
- Warm without being flirty.
- Specific about product facts, options, timing, privacy, and tradeoffs.
- Honest when something depends on supplier confirmation.
- Protective of buyers who are comparing expensive products privately.
- Comfortable using adult commerce terms where needed, but never gratuitous.

Avoid:

- Porn-style language.
- Fake intimacy.
- Fake reviews or invented buyer stories.
- Medical, legal, or psychological certainty.
- Overpromising shipping, customization, durability, or realism.
- Making AI-generated visuals sound like real product photography.

## Anti-ChatGPT Style Rules

The writing should feel like it came from someone who has handled expensive adult-product purchases, supplier options, shipping questions, and private buyer concerns before. It should not sound like a generic AI explainer.

### Ban These Patterns

Avoid phrases and structures that make the copy sound generated:

- `In today's world`
- `When it comes to`
- `At the end of the day`
- `It is important to note`
- `This comprehensive guide`
- `Whether you're a beginner or an experienced...`
- `Dive into`
- `Unlock`
- `Elevate your experience`
- `Game-changer`
- `Perfect for everyone`
- `Seamless`
- `Boasts`
- `Crafted to perfection`
- `Ultimate guide` unless it is truly a major pillar page
- Repeating the keyword in every paragraph
- Starting multiple sections with the same sentence shape
- Ending sections with generic summaries like `By understanding these factors, you can make an informed decision.`
- Em dashes. Use a period, comma, colon, or parentheses instead.
- `Not just X, but Y` / `not only X, it is Y` / `more than just X` constructions.
- Contrived contrast lines like `This is not just about buying a doll. It is about confidence, privacy, and control.`

### Preferred Sentence Rhythm

Use short and medium sentences. Let one plain sentence carry the point, then follow with a concrete buyer detail.

Good:

`TPE usually wins on softness and price. The tradeoff is care: it needs a more careful cleaning and powdering routine, and the exact feel still depends on the brand's formula.`

Avoid:

`When it comes to choosing between TPE and silicone, it is important to understand the unique benefits and considerations of each material so you can make an informed decision.`

### Use Specific Buyer Context

Every page should include practical details a real buyer would care about:

- What changes the final price.
- What affects handling and storage.
- What must be confirmed before checkout.
- Which claims depend on supplier confirmation.
- What is different for ready-to-ship vs custom.
- What a buyer should ask support before paying.
- Where the product page, collection, policy, or support page gives the next answer.

### Avoid Over-Polishing

Do not make every paragraph symmetrical. Do not make every section sound like a landing page. Use occasional plain, grounded phrasing:

- `That is the part worth checking before you pay.`
- `This is where buyers get surprised.`
- `Do not assume the same option exists on every body in the brand.`
- `If timing matters, ask before checkout.`
- `The product page should win over the brochure copy.`

Use these sparingly. The tone should stay premium, not casual or jokey.

## Editorial Promise

Every article should help the buyer answer three questions:

1. What should I compare before buying?
2. What can go wrong if I assume too much?
3. How does DollWow help me verify the product, price, options, and delivery path?

Jesse should avoid:

- Clinical certainty without evidence.
- Therapy language.
- Moralizing.
- Sensational language.
- Overly explicit phrasing.

Alex should avoid:

- Fanboy language.
- Fake personal ownership stories unless a real firsthand note is provided.
- Declaring a model the `best` without criteria.
- Treating factory images as proof of every final custom build.

## Content Claim Rules

### Allowed

- `This product is listed as ready to ship after stock confirmation.`
- `Supplier options can vary by model.`
- `DollWow confirms timing and available approval materials before fulfillment.`
- `TPE is usually softer, while silicone is often firmer and more durable depending on formulation.`
- `Check local law before ordering if you have concerns.`

### Not Allowed

- `Guaranteed delivery by [date]` unless operationally guaranteed.
- `Doctor-approved`, `medically safe`, or `body-safe` without evidence.
- `Legal in every state`.
- `Reviewed by thousands of customers` unless true.
- `Factory authorized` unless provenance is confirmed.
- Founder identities or ownership claims in Jesse/Alex bylines unless approved by the founders.
