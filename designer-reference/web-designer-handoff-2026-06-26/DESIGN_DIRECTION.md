# Design Direction

## Brand Feel

DollWow is a premium adult commerce storefront for realistic companion dolls. Most vendors in the category sell similar products with similar factory photos, so DollWow needs to stand out through buying experience, trust, clarity, customization, comparison tools, and support.

The site should feel:

- Premium and discreet
- Warm, intimate, and emotionally aware
- Clear and practical
- Trustworthy without sounding corporate
- More boutique than marketplace
- More helpful than flashy

## Visual Theme

The current direction is Noir Boudoir:

- deep oxblood, espresso, charcoal, black-brown backgrounds
- dusty rose and muted blush section shifts
- warm champagne/caramel CTA tones
- ivory text
- restrained green only for successful/selected/approved states
- thin premium borders rather than heavy cards
- fewer large rounded rectangles; corners can be softened, but not cartoonish

Avoid:

- hot pink/magenta adult-store styling
- neon club visuals
- childish rounded cards
- overly glossy “AI startup” gradients
- fake social proof
- generic stock-photo trust badges

## Copy Tone

Keep language simple. This is ecommerce, not a SaaS dashboard.

Good:

- “Factory photos before shipment”
- “Plain-box shipping”
- “Price match review”
- “Ready to ship”
- “Custom order”
- “Ask our team”
- “Options selected”

Avoid:

- “Your path”
- “Order path”
- “Stock path”
- “Flow”
- “Review layer”
- “Queued for workflow”
- “Internal verification model”
- “Data-backed enrichment”

## Product Page Priorities

The PDP needs to sell the exact doll first:

1. Product name, specs, price, and delivery state
2. Strong image gallery
3. Detailed measurements and head/body details
4. Customization studio
5. Trust and ordering details
6. Related products

The lower page should not become a wall of text. Use visual modules, iconography, small diagrams, progressive disclosure, and short copy blocks.

## Customizer Priorities

The customization studio is the signature experience. It should feel like a premium builder, not a form.

Keep:

- category navigation
- option thumbnails
- selected state
- next/back progression
- final build summary
- option price changes
- mobile usability

Improve:

- hierarchy between option categories, active option, selected options, and price
- visual richness of the central preview area
- summary state after all selections
- clarity around included vs paid options

## Trust Priorities

Trust should be visible, but not noisy.

Important promises:

- private checkout and billing
- plain-box shipping
- factory photos/videos before shipment for custom builds
- arrival/replacement support for major delivery problems
- free repair kit support for minor shipping wear
- 30-day best-price review after purchase
- human support before and after ordering

Represent these as clean buyer-protection modules, not legal copy.
