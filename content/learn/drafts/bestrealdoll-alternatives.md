## The "Best" Claim Needs Context

A best-seller or best-doll page can be useful if it helps buyers narrow choices. It is less useful if every product sounds equally perfect. For a high-consideration doll purchase, the best option is the one that fits the buyer's space, privacy needs, handling comfort, budget, and order timeline.

Alex's practical rule: if a page says a doll is best, it should also explain who it is best for.

That means checking:

- Height and carrying weight
- Material and surface finish
- Body type and measurements
- Stock status and delivery path
- Customization availability
- Factory photo or final approval expectations where applicable
- Support access before checkout

## Common Mistakes

### Trusting a best list without checking fit

A top-ranked doll can still be the wrong choice if it is too heavy, too tall, too difficult to store, or built on the wrong order path.

### Comparing a warehouse product to a custom build

Warehouse listings often prioritize speed and fixed configuration. Custom builds prioritize choices and production review. Compare one path at a time.

### Ignoring total delivered value

The product price is only one layer. Shipping, upgrades, custom choices, support, and timing all affect the real decision.
